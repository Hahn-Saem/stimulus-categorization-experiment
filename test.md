# Stimulus Categorization Experiment - Manual Test Description
### What to Expect: (as of Project Check-in #1):
The user should expect to see poorly formatted instructions appearing on the screen after opening the experiment (via `python run_experiment.py`) in the terminal. 

As of 4/3/26, the instructions are poorly formatted, with some of the words appearing beyond the size of the screen. From this point onward, the participant will be in the driver's seat, controlling the pace of the remainder of the experiment by clicking either the SPACE bar, 'f' key, or 'j' key. 

Per the instructions, the participant should be able to click the SPACE bar to begin the experiment. After clicking SPACE bar, the screen should now display an image of a dog on the screen. By pressing the 'f' or 'j' key, the participant can proceed to the next part of the experiment, where a picture of a shoe will be displayed.

Finally, a message notifying the participant that they have completed the experiment should appear on the screen. 

To exit out of the program, the user will click the SPACE bar.

Note: The final version of this project is expected to behave similarly. However, there will be more intentionally chosen stimuli, the stimuli will be randomly displayed, and the participant's data will be automatically saved at the end of the experiment. 

Ultimately, the program will run as anticipated if the instructions appear on the screen when they should (at the beginning and the end), images appear in pairs on the screen at random orders, the program moves on when the proper key is pressed, and the participant data saves.
